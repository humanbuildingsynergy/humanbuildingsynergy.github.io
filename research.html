Mission

The Human Building Synergy Laboratory (HUBS) paves the way for smart and sustainable built environments through innovative solutions to Architectural & Civil Engineering.

In so doing, HUBS builds on the fourth industrial revolution's advances to be creative & revolutionary, and push the boundaries of Architectural & Civil Engineering.

  • How can buildings self-adaptively condition their spaces?
  • How can buildings non-invasively and effortlessly collect occupants' data?
  • How can individuals effectively communicate with buildings?
These endless research questions are what HUBS addresses.

Healthy Buildings

Research questions:
 How can we quantitatively measure building's performance from the occupant health perspectives?
Can we quantify health benefits by buildings in a monetary manner and consider them as part of life cycle costs?
 Description:
 This research develops a excel-based tool that quantifies and monetizes the health benefits by improving the building performance with respect to temperature, humidity, indoor air quality (e.g., Particular Matter 2.5).
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
PI: Kevin Keene (previously Nora W. Esram, Ph.D.); Peers: Erik Mets, Alison Delgado, Hannah Rabinowitz Ph.D., Luke Troup, Ph.D.; Sponsor: the Department of Energy

Human-Aware Environments for Distributed Building Systems Operations

Research questions:
 How can we feasibly (i.e., non invasively) measure occupant's physiological responses to develop personal thermal comfort models?
 Description:
 Through innovative sensing approaches using RGB video images, Doppler radar sensors, and heat flux sensors, this research introduces feasibly solutions to capture individual physiological mechanisms as support to perosnal thermal comfort models.
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
PI: Farrokh Jazizadeh, Ph.D.; Sponsor: National Science Foundation.

Smart Control
SRP: Benefits of Smart Electrical Panels for SRP Sustainability Goals

Research questions:
 To what extent do smart electric panles (SEPs) support Salt River Project (SRP)'s demand response (DR) goals?
 Description:
 We intend to analyze circult-level power data collected from actual households to demonstrate the DR potentials through SEPs.
Project details:
 PI: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
Sponsor: Salt River Project.

ARPA-E SENSOR project: Occupant-centric HVAC control

Research questions:
 How can we examine occupancy sensors systemactially in a residential building and what'd be the energy impact of having an occupancy-centric HVAC operation?
 Description:
 While collaborating with sensor developers, occupancy-centric HVAC controls were implemented at the PNNL Lab Homes (figure) in Richland, WA, and examined the sensor and HVAC performances!
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
PI: Zheng O'Neill at TAMU, Co-PI: Jian Zhang, Ph.D.; Sponsor: the Department of Energy.

Occupancy schedule development through a large smart thermostat dataset

Research questions:
 Can smart home devices like smart thermostats could contribute to BEM?
What are the impactful parameters to determine occupancy schedules in residential buildings?
 Description:
 Benefiting from a large-scale ecobee dataset, collected from the Donate Your Data program, data-driven residential occupancy schedules were identified and a tool that generates stochastic residential occupancy schedules is developed. More details will be released soon!
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
Advisor: Tianzhen Hong, Ph.D. & Farrokh Jazizadeh, Ph.D.; Sponsor: National Science Foundation.

Building Energy Modeling
SRP: Towards All-Electric Buildings in the SRP Territory.

Description:
 This project aims to support development of a rebate program for non-residential building electrification strategies by analyzing potential impacts on energy demand and non-energy benefits, i.e., improved indoor air quality, in the SRP territory.
Project details: PI: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
Co-PI: Chris Chaeha Lim, Ph.D.; Sponsor: Salt River Project

ASHRAE Standard 140 Support.

Description:
 This project aims to update ASHRAE Standard 140 by evaluating different BEM tools.
PNNL was responsible for EnergyPlus and python scripts and functions were developed to modify idf files (through epJSON files.)
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
PI: Jian Zhang, Ph.D.; Peers: Doug Maddox, Shaojie Wang, Ph.D.; Sponsor: the Department of Energy.

EnergyPlus Development

Description:
 EnergyPlus is rather a standard BEM engine, which has been sponsored & developed by the Department of Energy for ages.
Dr. Jung was involved in the E+ development project (9.6 version and 2022 version) and developed several features, including 'Standard62.1SimplifiedProcedure', 'SetpointManager:SystemNodeReset:Temperature', and so on.
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
PI: Jian Zhang, Ph.D.; Peers: Jeremy Lerond, Xuechen Lei, Ph.D.; Sponsor: the Department of Energy.

BECP: Building-space-specific load & schedule development

Description:
 ASHRAE90.1 introduces building-level schedules and loads and such representations do not reflect unpredictable natures in BEM.
Hence, this research aims to develop building-space-specific loads and schedules, which will demonstrate diversity in loads and schedules (occupancy, lighting, plug loads) in each space & building type.
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
Task Lead: Jian Zhang, Ph.D.,; Peers: Yunyang Ye, Ph.D.; Sponsor: the Department of Energy.

BECP: ASHRAE 90.1 Appendix G baseline model development

Description:
 ASHRAE 90.1 Appendix G elaborates on the performance rating method for code compliance and the baseline model was developed by PNNL.
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
Task Lead: Chitra Nambiar; Peers: Doug Maddox, Xuechen Lei, Ph.D., Harshil Nagda; Sponsor: the Department of Energy.

Human-Aware Environments for Distributed Building Systems Operations

Research questions:
 How can we better optimized individually preferred environments in a shared (i.e., multi-occupancy) space?
 Description:
 This research identifies the impact of addressing thermal comfort sensitivity in optimizing preferred temperatures by multiple occupants, and analyzed the energy impacts of having diverse multi-occupancy spaces (e.g., different individuals, numbers, etc.).
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
PI: Farrokh Jazizadeh, Ph.D.; Sponsor: National Science Foundation.

Multi-sector Electricity Load mOdeliNg (MELON)

Description:
 This project aims to develop a U.S. Northwest demand load profiling tool addressing residential & commercial buildings, industry, transportation, and public service sectors and analyze the energy and environmental impacts of electrification.
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
PI: Yunyang Ye, Ph.D.; Peers: Xuechen Lei, Ph.D., Xiaoqi Liu, Ph.D., Eli Brock; Sponsor: Pacific Northwest National Laboratory.

BECP: Grid-interactive effective buildings

Description:
 This research aims to update ASHRAE 90.1 with regard to demand response. Using Time-Of-Use rates publisehd by DOE, the energy impact of having a battery system in a commercial building was analyzed as the steppingstone.
Project details:
 HUBS member: Wooyoung Jung, Ph.D.
Task Lead: Ellen Franconi, Ph.D.; Peers: Xuechen Lei, Ph.D., Vrushali Mendon; Sponsor: the Department of Energy.
